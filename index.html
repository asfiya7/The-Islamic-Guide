DISCOVER ISLAM

Faith/Shahada

Belief in the Unity of Allah & Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as Allah’s messenger.

Prayer/Salah

The act of worshipping Allah five times a day throughout your lifetime.

Alms/Zakat

Sharing a certain percentage of your wealth with less fortunate every year.

Fasting/Roza

Fasting from dawn to dusk for 30 days in the holy month of Ramadan.

Pilgrimage/Hajj

A spiritual duty that every Muslim must offer once in their life, if able.

12 Sunnah Practices for a Productive Life

1. Sleeping and Waking Up Early

Our sleeping pattern plays a significant role in our daily effectiveness. The hours after Fajr are among the most blessed. Start your day with Fajr prayer to gain Barakah and the right mindset. To wake up early, it is vital to sleep early for adequate rest.

2. Having a Pleasant Smile Always

The Prophet (peace be upon him) frequently smiled. Smiling lifts the mood and spreads positivity. Follow this Sunnah to foster relationships and maintain a positive outlook.

3. Using the Siwak

The Prophet (peace be upon him) emphasized oral hygiene with the siwak. It is an affordable and natural way to maintain clean and fresh teeth, pleasing Allah.

4. Oiling Hair

Regularly oiling your hair prevents greying and strengthens it. This Sunnah promotes relaxation and saves time and money while improving hair health.

5. Maintaining the 1/3rd Rule in Eating

The Prophet (peace be upon him) advised eating one-third food, one-third drink, and leaving one-third for air. Avoid overeating to prevent laziness and stay energetic.

6. Speaking Good or Keeping Silent

The Prophet (peace be upon him) taught us to speak good or remain silent. Avoid gossip and use your time for productive actions.

7. Doing Hijama (Cupping)

Hijama removes toxins, alleviates migraines, and reduces stress. It is a therapeutic Sunnah practiced by the Prophet (peace be upon him).

8. Visiting the Sick

Visiting the sick fosters love and reminds us of the blessing of health. Make the effort to comfort those who are ill and seek Barakah.

9. Sitting When Eating/Drinking

Sit down to eat and drink mindfully. This prevents overeating, promotes healthy digestion, and strengthens relationships through shared meals.

10. Sleeping on Your Right Side

The Prophet (peace be upon him) slept on his right side. This habit is key to a fruitful and productive morning.

11. Saying Salam/Bismillah Before Entering a House

Mention Allah’s Name when entering your home to keep shaytan away. This habit fosters a productive and peaceful household.

12. Untying the Three Knots

Wake up for Fajr to undo the knots tied by shaytan. Praise Allah, perform wudu, and pray to start your day in good spirits.
