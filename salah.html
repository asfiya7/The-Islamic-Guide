Salah - The Second Pillar of Islam

A Daily Prayer

The second pillar of Islam, the daily five prayers (Salah), symbolize a believer's submission to Allah عَزَّ وَجَلَّ and serve as a public display of their commitment to Islam. Salah holds immense significance, ranking after the Shahada, and through this practice, Muslims acknowledge their dependence on Allah, seeking guidance, forgiveness, and spiritual nourishment.

What is Salah in Islam?

The obligation to perform the second pillar of Islam is one of the most emphasized duties in Islam. Muslims, regardless of gender, are required to perform these prayers at their designated times, as prescribed by Sharia (Islamic law). Before each prayer, ablution (Wudu) must be performed correctly.

The five daily prayers are:

  • Fajr: Before sunrise
  • Dhuhr: Midday, after the sun's highest point
  • Asr: Evening
  • Maghrib: After sunset
  • Isha: Night

The Quran and Hadith emphasize the virtues of Salah, warning of severe consequences for neglecting it. The Quran states: "O believers! Let not your wealth or your children make you negligent from the remembrance of Allah, and whoever does that, they are the losers." (Surat al-Munafiqun, Ayah 9)

Virtues of Salah

Salah is a comprehensive spiritual exercise that carries numerous virtues and benefits. Here are 25 virtues of Salah:

  • Salah pleases Allah عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.
  • It is a Sunnah of the blessed Prophets.
  • Salah is a lamp in the grave.
  • It protects against punishment in the grave and Hellfire.
  • Salah provides Noor (light) and is the key to Paradise.
  • It brings blessings, comfort, and forgiveness.
  • Through Salah, believers receive guidance, mercy, and answered Du’as.
  • It strengthens the soul and purifies from sins.
  • Salah protects against indecency and bad deeds.
  • It makes the scale of good deeds heavy.
  • The greatest blessing: Seeing Allah عَزَّ وَجَلَّ on the Day of Judgement.

Quranic Verses on Salah

  • "Establish Salah for My remembrance." (Surah Ta-Ha, Ayah 14)
  • "Indeed, Salah has been made obligatory upon the Muslims at fixed times." (Surah al-Nisa, Ayah 103)
  • "Good deeds erase evil deeds." (Surah Hood, Ayah 114)

Importance of Salah in Hadith

"The one who secures his Salah, the Salah will be Nur, evidence, and salvation for him on the Day of Judgement." (Musnad Ahmad)

It is said, "Salah washes away sins the way water washes away dirt." (Hadith)

Salah - A Purifying Practice

Prophet Isa عَـلَيْـهِ الـسَّـلَام observed a bird cleansing itself five times, symbolizing the purification achieved through the five daily prayers.

First Question in Afterlife

"On the Day of Judgement, the very first question that will be asked to man out of his deeds will be about Salah." (Hadith)
